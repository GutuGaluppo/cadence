Why Cadence

A timer that stays out of your way.

Task-aware sessions

Attach focus and break durations directly to the task you are working on instead of forcing one global timing rule for everything.

Local-first by default

Your settings and tasks stay on your machine. The product is shaped around fast local persistence, not around sign-up funnels.

Compact desktop flow

Tray access, a small footprint, and a focused single-window layout keep the app available without turning it into a second workspace.

How it works

From intention to rhythm in three steps.

01

Pick the task that matters now

Choose one task and let it define the working session instead of mentally juggling a full backlog while the clock is already running.

02

Stay with the session

Cadence handles the timer, transitions, and persistent state so you can stay inside the work instead of managing the tool.

03

Build momentum over the day

Completed cycles stay attached to tasks, giving you a clearer picture of where your attention actually went.

FAQ

Short answers before launch.

Does Cadence require an account?

No. The current product direction is local-first and account-free.

Where does my data live?

Settings are stored locally and tasks are persisted in a local SQLite database on the device.

Can I customize work and break durations?

Yes. Global settings exist, and active tasks can also carry their own timing profile.
